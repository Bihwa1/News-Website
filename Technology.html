PH addressing persistent cyber attacks tied to WPS dispute

Priam Nepomuceno

Published: February 19, 2026, 2:17 pm

MANILA - A ranking official of the Armed Forces of the Philippines (AFP) on Thursday said the country is facing "persistent cyber attacks" linked to its territorial dispute with China regarding the West Philippine Sea (WPS). AFP Cyber Command chief Brig. Gen. Joey Fontiveros, however, assured the public that government measures are in place to address issues arising from these threats. He said the Department of National Defense and the AFP view cybersecurity as integral to national security, given the reliance on information systems for territorial integrity and economic stability. "Government responses include the National Cyber Security Plan, which emphasizes advanced threat assessment, capacity building and closing skills gaps to bolster resilience," he said at a Stratbase Institute forum dubbed "Navigating Digital Crossroads: Advancing Cybersecurity and Democratic Resilience in the Indo-Pacific."

The military official said these threats have been intensified, contributing to a broader global risk profile where misinformation, disinformation, and cyber insecurity rank among top short and long-term concerns. "From a security and defense viewpoint, the country faces persistent cyber attacks linked to territorial disputes in the WPS with China-based malicious actors deploying malware, DDOS (distributed denial-of-service) attacks, data leaks and website compromises to advance Beijing's regional ambition," Fontiveros said. This year, Fontiveros said the Philippines' cybersecurity landscape is characterized by escalating threats driven by geopolitical tensions, rapid digital transformation and a sophisticated adversary environment, particularly from state-sponsored actors.

While the Philippines has detected foreign attempts to access intelligence data, the AFP official said no major breaches have been confirmed, though he underscored vulnerabilities in critical infrastructure like healthcare, transportation and power grids. "Overall, the landscape demands a shift from reactive to proactive defenses, integrating AI (artificial intelligence)-powered detection and multilateral cooperation to counter hybrid threats in a digitally interconnected region," Fontiveros said. To better secure the country's cyberspace, Fontiveros said the AFP has prioritized cyber defense as a core component of its modernization under the Comprehensive Archipelagic Defense Concept as the military now recognizes cyber threats as equivalent to physical ones in modern warfare.

Your own voice could be your biggest privacy threat. How can we stop AI technologies exploiting it?

Drew Turney

Published: February 21, 2026

If you know what to listen for, a person's voice can tell you about their education level, emotional state and even profession and finances — more so than you could imagine. Now, scientists posit that technology in the form of voice-to-text recordings can be used in price gouging, unfair profiling, harassment or stalking. While humans might be attuned to more obvious cues such as fatigue, nervousness, happiness and so on, computers can do the same, but with far more information, and much faster. A new study claims intonation patterns or your choice of words can reveal everything from your personal politics to the presence of health or medical conditions. The research, published Nov. 19, 2025 in the journal Proceedings of the IEEE, highlights a grave concern for the technology's capability in privacy and unfair profiling.

While voice processing and recognition technology present opportunities, Aalto University's speech and language technology associate professor Tom Bäckström, lead author of the study, sees the potential for serious risks and harms. If a corporation understands your economic situation or needs from your voice, for instance, it opens the door to price gouging, like discriminatory insurance premiums. And when voices can reveal details like emotional vulnerability, gender and other personal details, cybercriminals or stalkers can identify and track victims across platforms and expose them to extortion or harassment. These are all details we transmit subconsciously when we speak and which we unconsciously respond to before anything else.
