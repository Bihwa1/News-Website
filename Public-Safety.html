3 weather systems to bring rains over parts of PH

Gov PH

Published: February 22, 2026, 6:26 am

MANILA - The shear line, northeast monsoon or amihan, and easterlies will bring rains over parts of the country, the weather bureau said Sunday. The Eastern Visayas, Central Visayas, Misamis Oriental, Camiguin, Surigao del Norte, and Dinagat Islands will have cloudy skies with scattered rains and isolated thunderstorms due to the shear line. The Philippine Atmospheric, Geophysical and Astronomical Services Administration (PAGASA), in its 4 a.m. advisory, said flash floods or landslides may occur due to moderate to at times heavy rains in these areas. Cagayan, Isabela, and Aurora will experience cloudy skies with light rains while Metro Manila, the rest of Luzon, and the rest of Visayas will have isolated light rains due to amihan. The rest of Mindanao will have partly cloudy to cloudy skies with isolated rain showers or thunderstorms caused by easterlies. Moderate to strong northeast winds and moderate to rough seas will prevail over the eastern sections of Southern Luzon and Visayas. The rest of the country will have light to moderate east to northeast winds and slight to moderate coastal waters. (PNA)

MMDA urges public to use mass transport as traffic jams expected during Christmas season

Aaron Recuenco

Published: Dec 10, 2025 05:31 pm

The Metropolitan Manila Development Authority (MMDA) urged commuters to use mass transportation system to decongest roads in Metro Manila of motor vehicles, which is one of the major reasons behind monstrous traffic jams during the Christmas season. MMDA chairman Don Artes said the volume of motor vehicles on major thoroughfares, particularly EDSA, has already exceeded its capacity and the increase in the volume during Holiday season is causing traffic congestion. In a press briefing, Artes revealed that around 450,000 motor vehicles were recorded along EDSA alone, which he said, is more than the 250,000 capacity.
