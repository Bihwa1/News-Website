Finance Sec. Go: PH economy to bounce back, to grow by at least 5% in 2026

Jekki Pascual, ABS-CBN News

Published: Feb 02, 2026 06:37 PM

MANILA -- Finance Secretary Frederick Go is optimistic the Philippine economy can bounce back again this year, even saying the country's gross domestic product will hit at least the low end of the government's revised target of 5 to 6 percent. “For 2026, we will bounce back and we will definitely have a GDP of at least 5 percent. Of course we're working towards a 5 plus percent GDP growth for 2026,” he said. Go, speaking at the Foreign Correspondents Association of the Philippines, said the country's growth in 2025 was hampered by several factors, both domestic issues and external risks. “Flood control expose, the global events like the reciprocal tariffs as well as natural disasters that hit the Philippines. We're so used to typhoons, but I guess we're not used to earthquakes, and all these events in the second half of last year affected our GDP growth,” Go said. One of the things that could help lift the economy up, Go said, is the resolution of the flood control scandal and the return of the stolen money to the government.

“The solution is simply prosecution, restitution, and genuine reforms. The people want to see people punished and go to jail,” he said. He added that the country's strong fundamentals, like easing inflation and good credit ratings will also help beef up the economy again. Several reforms have also started, like new laws or amendments to laws have been passed— like the New Public-Private Partnership Code, Investors' Lease Act, Accelerated and Reformed Right-of-Way Act, among others. He also says the government is spending more efficiently this year. The government is set to spend P1.4 trillion in the first quarter to stimulate the economy. “Reminding all my fellow cabinet members about smart spending, spending fficiently, and making sure that we spend projects with the highest multiplier effects,” Go said.

Stock Market News for Feb 18, 2026

Zacks Equity Research

Published: Wed, February 18, 2026 at 5:44 PM GMT+8

U.S. stock markets closed higher on Tuesday. Stock prices were muted yesterday as major indexes were mostly sidetracked. Strong fourth-quarter 2025 earnings results failed to enthuse market participants due to artificial intelligence (AI)-related disruption fears. However, all three major stock indexes ended in positive territory.

AI Setback Continues

Investors have been rotating out of tech stocks on growing concerns over the downsides of AI stocks. On Tuesday, the selloff continued as fears grew over the potential of AI stocks compared to the billions of dollars pumped into the sector. Some sections of the stock market have suffered this year following the rollout of AI tools that threaten to copy what they do or eat into their profit margins. Financial stocks, especially, have been feeling the heat on concerns that AI could hamper wealth management businesses. Several other sectors, such as media, trucking and real estate, also suffered due to AI disruption fears. Major software developers are also bearing the brunt of AI fear as market participants remained highly concerned that AI tools will replace industry-specific software that these developers are providing.

Strong Earnings Results

CNH Industrial N.V. CNH came up with quarterly adjusted earnings of $0.19 per share, beating the Zacks Consensus Estimate of $0.11 per share. This compares to earnings of $0.15 per share a year ago. The company posted revenues of $5.16 billion, surpassing the Zacks Consensus Estimate by 2.74%. This compares to year-ago revenues of $4.88 billion.

Krystal Biotech Inc. KRYS reported quarterly adjusted earnings of $1.70 per share, beating the Zacks Consensus Estimate of $1.62 per share. This compares to earnings of $1.52 per share a year ago. The company posted revenues of $107.11 million, missing the Zacks Consensus Estimate of $108.61 million. This compares to year-ago revenues of $91.14 million.
